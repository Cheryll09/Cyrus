What is Adaptation?

📌 Definition

Adaptation is a special feature or behaviour that helps a living thing survive in its habitat (environment).

Every living thing has adaptations that suit it perfectly to where it lives. These are not learned — they are inherited (passed down from parents).

Types of Adaptation

TypeWhat it meansExample
StructuralA physical feature of the body — shape, part, coveringThick fur of a polar bear
BehaviouralSomething the animal does — an action or behaviourCamels storing water in their hump fat
⚡ PSLE Trick
If you can see it on the body → structural. If it's something the animal DOES → behavioural.

The Golden Rule for Answering

📝 Answer Structure

"The [feature/behaviour] allows/helps the [animal] to [survive/obtain food/escape predators/etc.] in its [habitat]."

Always link the adaptation to survival. Never just describe what the feature looks like — explain why it helps the animal survive.

❌ Weak Answer
A cactus has a thick stem. (No link to survival!)
✅ Strong Answer
A cactus stores water in its thick stem, allowing it to survive in the hot, dry desert where there is little rainfall.

⭐ PSLE Exam Tips for Adaptation

Use these strategies during your exam to score full marks.

🏷️

Always Label the Type

State whether it is a structural or behavioural adaptation if the question asks. Don't skip this — it's often 1 mark.

🔗

Link to Survival

Never just describe the feature. Always explain how it helps the animal survive, obtain food, or avoid predators.

🌡️

Mention the Habitat Condition

Connect to the environment: "in the dry desert", "in cold arctic waters", "in the dark deep ocean." This shows understanding.

📏

Match Marks to Points

If a question is worth 2 marks, give 2 separate adaptation points. Each mark = one feature + one function.

🚫

Avoid Vague Words

Don't say "to help it survive." Be specific: "to reduce water loss", "to attract prey", "to camouflage from predators."

🔄

Structural vs Behavioural

Structural = body part/covering you can SEE.
Behavioural = something the animal DOES (action/habit).
